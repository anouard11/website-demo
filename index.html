My Teaching Philosophy

A Caring Parent Approach

Over time, and with the growth of many amazing sources of knowledge, I have come to believe that students don't just need a teacher who simply transfers information. Since they have access to so many rich resources, what they really need is a figure like a caring parent—someone who understands their deeper feelings, guides them through difficulties, and helps them navigate the challenges of modern life.

This belief puts a lot of responsibility on me as a teacher. I cannot limit myself to just my subject. I must also grow in related areas like educational psychology and sociology so that I can support and guide my students in the best way possible.

Growth Mindset

Every mistake is a learning opportunity, and every challenge is a chance to grow stronger.

Collaborative Learning

Learning is enhanced when students work together, share ideas, and support each other's journey.

Individual Excellence

Recognizing that each student is unique with different strengths, interests, and learning styles.

Reflection on My Micro-Teaching

  • Date: 16/07/2025
  • Level: Elementary
  • Lesson: Vocabulary – My Home and Daily Routine
  • Materials: Pictures, recorded audios, flashcards
  • Time: 15 minutes

This was my first time teaching a class. Although I had never taught before, I had some experience explaining things to people in everyday life, mostly adults. That helped me feel more confident.

The lesson went well overall, even though the time was short. If I had to rate it, I'd give it a 7 out of 10. I focused on helping students learn new vocabulary in a simple and clear way.

Professor Bakks gave me helpful feedback that made me notice things I hadn't thought about before. His advice really helped me improve and gave me a better idea of how to teach more effectively in the future.

My Evaluation of the TESOL Training Program

To be honest, there's a lot I could say about the TESOL training program offered by the American TESOL Academy. Overall, it has been a good experience. Here are some of the main pros and cons:

✅ Pros:

  • Qualified Trainers – The instructors are knowledgeable and have real experience in teaching English.
  • Different Trainer Perspectives – I really like Professor Qasser's idea of bringing several trainers into the program. This gives us different styles, ideas, and experiences.
  • Supportive Environment – The trainers and classmates are helpful and encouraging.
  • Many Other Strengths – The program also provides good resources and helps improve teaching skills in general.

❌ Cons:

  • Not Enough Real Teaching Practice – We didn't get much chance to teach real ESL students. Teaching is something you learn best by doing, not just by studying theory.
  • Few Microteaching Sessions – There weren't enough chances to practice teaching, even with classmates.

Advice for Readers

Learn How to Learn, Think, and Analyze

These are very important skills that you will need in any field. Knowing how to learn and think critically will save you a lot of time and help you navigate any situation more effectively.

Read, Read, and Read

You can't imagine how much experience and knowledge you can gain from reading. Imagine living 70 years and writing down all your experiences, solutions, and insights in a book — reading lets you learn from others' "books" without waiting decades.

Values Come First, Then Life

In today's fast-paced and competitive world, it's easy to lose sight of your core values. Sometimes people sacrifice their principles for money or career advancement, but this is the worst thing you can do. Always put your values first, then your life and career will follow.
